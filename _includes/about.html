Our Founding Story

More than twenty five years ago in the mid 1990's, I was teaching tech courses in Computer Graphics in Toronto, Canada. I liked it, but my real interest was in the Web, which was growing rapidly. But in the colleges and universities, no courses, or very primitive instruction on how to use a browser was almost all there was.

I worked with a private company to develop a comprehensive Web instruction course, and soon the Canadian Government kickstarted our initial course into full 14-week, intensive and comprehensive Webmaster course. Most significantly, it allowed me to teach my students what interested me most: programming computers to do amazing things on the web.

In everything but name, we'd created the technology bootcamp, focused on bringing newbies, with no previous contact with computers, into the world of full-on web development. Others may have done the same; but I and my team had funding, an eager audience, but no reference points directing us to how anyone could be helped to use the web as a creative programming environment.

Our Mission

The Digitally Disenfranchised – 4Bn of us – lack the capacity to control their own digital destiny, first through exclusion, and then because they lack the education to direct technology to their own ends.

We're dedicated to researching, prototyping, and implementing solutions at the world's digital divide. Based in South East Asia, where digital disenfranchisement sits side by side with some of the fastest growing IT cultures, we're working with universities, NGOs, private enterprise and government to build a path to digital inclusion that the region – and the world's – digitally excluded can use to take digital control of their lives.
